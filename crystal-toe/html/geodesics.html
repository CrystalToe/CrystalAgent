WHAT YOU'RE SEEING

A particle orbiting a black hole in Einstein's general relativity. The orbit is not a closed ellipse — it precesses, tracing a rosette pattern. This precession is what made Einstein famous: he predicted Mercury's orbit precesses by 43 arcseconds per century, exactly matching the observed anomaly that Newton couldn't explain.

THE SLIDERS

L (angular momentum) — controls how close the particle gets. High L → wide orbit, slow precession. Low L → tight orbit, fast precession. Below L ≈ 3.46, there is no stable orbit — the particle plunges into the black hole. Drag it down and watch it happen.

E (energy) — controls whether the orbit is bound (E < 1) or escaping (E > 1). At E slightly below the V_eff barrier peak, the particle barely stays bound.

THE V_EFF PLOT

The curve on the right is the effective potential — the total "landscape" the particle moves through. The green dot shows where the particle currently is. The dashed line is the particle's energy. When the energy is below the peak, the particle is trapped. When it's above, the particle escapes or plunges.
