WHAT YOU'RE SEEING

A Schwarzschild black hole rendered in 3D. The black sphere is the event horizon at r_s = N_w · GM = 2GM. The faint blue ring is the photon sphere at N_c · GM = 3GM — where light itself orbits. The glowing disk starts at the innermost stable circular orbit at ISCO = χ · GM = 6GM.

The bright particle traces a real geodesic computed by WasmGR — a solution to Einstein's field equations in Schwarzschild spacetime. Watch it precess: each orbit rotates by δφ = χ·π·GM/a = 6πGM/a. This is what Mercury does — 43 arcseconds per century.

THE ACCRETION DISK

Matter can't orbit closer than the ISCO. Below r = 6GM = χ·GM, there are no stable orbits — everything plunges in. The disk's inner edge IS the Crystal's bond dimension. The disk particles orbit at Keplerian speeds: v = √(GM/r), faster closer in. The color shifts from blue (approaching) to red (receding) via gravitational_redshift(r_s, r).
