HOW IT WORKS

This uses the Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM), specifically the D2Q9 model. Instead of solving the Navier-Stokes equations directly, it simulates millions of fictitious particles bouncing on a lattice. The fluid behavior emerges from their collisions. Each cell has 9 velocity directions — one rest, four cardinal, four diagonal.

WHY 9 VELOCITIES?

In the Crystal Topos, 9 = N_c² = 3². The colour prime N_c = 3 gives us 3 spatial dimensions, and the D2Q9 lattice has N_c² velocity states per cell. This is not a coincidence — the lattice must have enough symmetry to recover the Navier-Stokes equations, and that symmetry requirement gives exactly N_c² in 2D.
