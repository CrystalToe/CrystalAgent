The Hierarchy, Read Off the Formula

The unified closed-form M(n) = Nw·[C(n,1) + C(n,2) + C(n,3)] + Nw·C(n,2)·𝟙(n≤Nc) with (Nw, Nc) = (2, 3) is not monolithic — it is a sum of three columns. Each column defines a stratum:

Low regime · n ≤ Nc = 3

M(n) = n(n+1)(n+2) / 3
cumulative 3D harmonic oscillator → {2, 8, 20}

Pure HO cumulation. Mayer–Jensen 1949 before spin–orbit. Agrees with experiment for the first three closures.

High regime · n ≥ 4

M(n) = n(n² + 5) / 3
Wigner spin–orbit correction → {28, 50, 82, 126}

Spin–orbit reshuffling kicks in. The pure-HO prediction at n=4 would be 40 — framework corrects to 28, matching observation.

The switch at exactly n = Nc = 3 is the same point where the pure harmonic oscillator fails experimentally. The column count Nc = 3 encodes physical-space dimension; the formula implements the HO → HO+SO transition as a structural consequence, not a tuned correction.

Closure Strength s(N) = Number of Reinforcing Layers

An integer that lies on multiple strata has multiple independent structural reasons to host a nuclear shell closure. The count is the framework's closure-strength prediction. This is the arithmetic closed-form analogue of the Dirac Confluence Mechanism (Ding et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 136 082501, 2026), which attributes robust magic numbers to the coincidence of spin and pseudospin symmetries.

What the Visual Shows

Each integer from 2 to 82 is plotted horizontally. The three layers (L0 primary gold, L1 pronic silver, L2 tertiary rose) are stacked vertically. A glowing shaft connects spheres representing the same integer across layers — the taller and thicker the shaft, the stronger the reinforcement.

Three golden spheres stacked vertically at N = 20 is the triple reinforcement that predicts ⁴⁰Ca's unique robustness (E(2⁺) = 3.904 MeV, the largest among first-row canonical closures). Two stacks at N = 28, 50, 82, 126 mark canonical SO-induced magic. Two stacks at N = 56 are the Ni-56 doubly-magic signature — strong but not canonical. Single rose spheres at N = 14, 16, 32, 34, 40, 64 are the emergent subshells of exotic neutron-rich nuclei. Dark red dots on the floor are the thirty even integers containing foreign primes — framework-forbidden, experimentally silent.
